
## Intro

This is a list of Recommended books (originally posted to the MeetUp discussion board).

### General

* [Clean Code](http://amzn.to/1Oqs2Rt) 
* [Code Complete](http://amzn.to/1TcZkQW) - Great for changing your mindset from throw-away school assignment code to readable, large-scale projects.
* [Growing Object-Oriented Software Guided By Tests](http://amzn.to/1XqS88U) - Slightly different perspective on TDD
* [Land the Tech Job You Love](https://pragprog.com/book/algh/land-the-tech-job-you-love) - Challenges ossified rituals with respect to resumes, interviewing, etc.
* [The Passionate Programmer](http://amzn.to/1TTD6pq) 
* [The Pragmatic Programmer](http://amzn.to/1YrHNrQ) - One of the most influential books of the last 15 years.
* [The Productive Programmer](http://nealford.com/books/productiveprogrammer) 
* [Release It!](https://pragprog.com/book/mnee/release-it) - Life with software, after it goes into production.
* [Understand the 4 Rules of Simple Design](https://leanpub.com/4rulesofsimpledesign)
* [Working Effectively with Legacy Code](http://amzn.to/1TcQkPd)

### Language Specific

* [Effective Java](http://amzn.to/1WxfVVj) - If you write Java, this is a must-read.
* [JavaScript: The Good Parts](http://amzn.to/1WxgiiK) - Throws you over the hump in JavaScript development
* [You Don't Know JS](https://github.com/getify/You-Dont-Know-JS)
